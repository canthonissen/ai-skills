# De Bono Thinking — Router

This is the entry point to a deliberate-thinking toolkit drawn from Edward de Bono's
*de Bono's Thinking Course*. Treat thinking here as a **practical skill you do**, not a
talent you either have or lack. The core move never changes: **deliberately direct
attention to a place you would otherwise skip**, one operation at a time, briefly
(about 2–3 minutes per tool).

Your job with this skill is to (1) frame the situation, (2) pick the right stage and
toolkit, and (3) run the relevant tool with the person — not to lecture about the
theory. Keep it light and useful.

## The one idea behind every tool: perception

De Bono's claim is that the mind is a **self-organizing pattern system**. From
experience it builds patterns and then runs down them automatically. That is why we are
fast and fluent at familiar things — and also why we stay stuck in the first way we see
something. Most everyday thinking errors are errors of *perception* (how we see the
situation), not errors of *logic* (reasoning correctly from how we see it).

So the tools do one of two things:

- **Broaden perception** — deliberately look in directions you'd naturally ignore
  (the attention-directing tools: PMI, CAF, C&S, OPV).
- **Cut across patterns** — deliberately break out of the established channel to find a
  new one (lateral thinking: challenge, alternatives, provocation, random input…).

You don't argue someone out of a bad pattern; you direct attention so a better
perception forms on its own.

## The spine: the five stages — TO · LO · PO · SO · GO

Every piece of thinking can be located in one of five stages. Name the stage you're in,
do that stage well, then move on. You can loop back any time.

| Stage | Say it as | The question | Toolkit to use |
|-------|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| **TO** | "Where am I going *to*?" | What is the aim / purpose / objective here? | `debono-decide` (AGO) |
| **LO** | "*Look* out" | What can I see? What do I know and need to know? | `debono-explore` (PMI, CAF, C&S, OPV) |
| **PO** | "*Po*ssibilities" | What *could* be — ideas, options, provocations? | `debono-create` (APC + lateral tools) |
| **SO** | "*So* what?" | Narrow down, weigh, conclude, choose. | `debono-decide` (FIP, values, decision) |
| **GO** | "*Go* do it" | What will I actually do, and what's the first step? | `debono-act` (operacy) |

A full think-through walks TO → LO → PO → SO → GO. A quick one might be a single tool.

**The backward lens (inversion).** Running parallel to all five stages is a sixth move:
*inversion* (`debono-invert`), the Jacobi/Munger discipline of thinking backward from
failure. At any stage, also ask the mirror question — not only "where am I going?" but
"where must I **not** end up, and what would lead there?" Invoke **debono-invert** whenever
a choice carries real downside, when someone asks "what could go wrong?", before committing
to a plan (a pre-mortem), or when a forward problem is stuck and its inverse is easier to
solve.

## Router — which toolkit do I need right now?

| The person's situation | Stage | Skill to invoke |
|------------------------|-------|-----------------|
| Reacting to a proposal / idea; "should we…?"; gut says yes or no | LO | **debono-explore** (start with PMI) |
| "What am I missing?"; before a plan or judgment | LO | **debono-explore** (CAF) |
| Weighing the future effects of a choice | LO | **debono-explore** (C&S) |
| A decision/conflict involving other people | LO | **debono-explore** (OPV) |
| Stuck, repetitive, "there must be a better way", need fresh ideas | PO | **debono-create** |
| Only one option on the table; need more | PO | **debono-create** (APC) |
| Need to define the real aim before anything else | TO | **debono-decide** (AGO) |
| Too many factors/options; what matters most? | SO | **debono-decide** (FIP) |
| Choosing between options; a real decision | SO | **debono-decide** |
| Have decided — now make it happen | GO | **debono-act** |
| Real downside or risk; "what could go wrong?"; high-stakes choice | LO/SO | **debono-invert** |
| About to commit to a plan; want a pre-mortem | GO | **debono-invert** |
| Forward problem is stuck ("how do we get more X?") | PO | **debono-invert** (Jacobi reframe) |
| Not sure / "just help me think about this" | all | start here, then route |

When in doubt, **start with debono-explore** (a quick PMI or CAF). De Bono's first
discipline is to look before you judge.

## Three things that run through all the tools

1. **Information & thinking.** Having more information is not a substitute for thinking.
   You almost never have complete information, so the skill is to think well with what
   you have, and to decide what's worth finding out. Don't let "we need more data" become
   an excuse to avoid thinking. (At the LO stage, always ask: *what do I know, and what
   do I need to know?*)

2. **Emotions & values.** Emotions and values are not the enemy of thinking — thinking
   exists to serve them. The trick is *sequence*: explore first, let emotion choose
   **after**. That is the whole point of PMI — to delay the instant emotional snap so the
   feeling, when it comes, is informed. Always make the values at stake explicit.

3. **Deliberate thinking.** This is the meta-skill. Set aside a clear "thinking spot,"
   run **one tool at a time**, keep each pass **brief** (2–3 min), and stay humble —
   thinking here is about being effective, not about being clever or winning. Separate
   "what do I think about this?" from "what kind of person am I?" — ego makes thinking
   worse.

## How to run a session

1. **Frame it.** In one sentence: what are we thinking about? (If the aim is fuzzy, do
   AGO from `debono-decide` first.)
2. **Pick the stage** using the spine and router above. Tell the person which tool you're
   going to run and why.
3. **Run one tool**, briefly and out loud (or on paper). Capture the output as a short
   list — don't polish.
4. **Decide the next move:** another tool, the next stage, or stop. Loop back freely.
5. **End on GO.** Thinking that doesn't change what you do or see was wasted. Name at
   least one concrete action or one changed perception.

## The full tool map

- **debono-explore** — PMI (Plus/Minus/Interesting), CAF (Consider All Factors), C&S
  (Consequence & Sequel), OPV (Other People's Views).
- **debono-create** — APC (Alternatives/Possibilities/Choices), Challenge, Concept
  Extraction, Random Input, Provocation (PO) + Movement, Reversal.
- **debono-decide** — AGO (Aims/Goals/Objectives), FIP (First Important Priorities),
  Values & Emotions check, the decision procedure.
- **debono-act** — Operacy: objective → steps → first action → who/when → monitor (C&S
  forward) → contingency.
- **debono-invert** — Inversion (Jacobi/Munger): Anti-goal, Failure Inversion, Jacobi
  reframe, Pre-mortem, Via Negativa. The backward lens that augments every stage.

A note on spirit: these are practical *attention-directing devices*, not a scientific
theory of the brain. They earn their keep by making you look where you wouldn't have
looked. Use them lightly and often.
